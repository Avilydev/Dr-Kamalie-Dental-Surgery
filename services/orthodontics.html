Orthodontics in Muizenberg & Strandfontein

Teeth do not always grow into place the way we expect. Some overlap, others drift slightly forward or sit at an angle. Over time, this can affect more than appearance. It can change how your bite feels and make certain areas harder to clean.

Orthodontic treatment focuses on guiding teeth into better positions so that your bite works comfortably and your smile feels balanced. Dr Emile Kamalie provides orthodontic care as a dentist in Muizenberg and Strandfontein, helping both teenagers and adults explore ways to improve tooth alignment.

Why does tooth alignment matter?

Straight teeth are not only about appearance. Alignment also plays a role in how evenly pressure is distributed when you chew.

When teeth sit too closely together or overlap, cleaning certain areas becomes more difficult. This may increase the chance of plaque buildup over time. That is why regular examinations and scale and polish appointments remain important before and during orthodontic care.

Proper alignment can also make it easier to maintain long-term oral health.

What orthodontic treatments are available?

Orthodontic care today often focuses on discreet solutions that fit into everyday life. One option offered is clear aligners, which gradually guide teeth into position through a series of custom trays.

Clear aligners are designed to be worn throughout the day and removed when eating and brushing. During your consultation, Dr Kamalie will assess your teeth and bite to determine whether this approach may be suitable for you.

Orthodontic treatment may help you address concerns such as:

  • Teeth that overlap or crowd together
  • Gaps that affect the appearance of your smile
  • Bites that feel uneven when chewing
  • Teeth that have shifted over time

Before starting orthodontic care, Dr Kamalie may check whether any restorative treatment is needed first. In some cases, a filling or a crown may help stabilise a tooth before alignment begins.

What happens after orthodontic treatment?

Once teeth have moved into their new positions, maintaining that alignment becomes the next step. Teeth can slowly shift over time if they are not supported.

This is where retainers become important. Retainers are designed to hold teeth in place after orthodontic treatment so that the results remain stable.

Some patients also benefit from mouthguards, particularly if they participate in sports. A well-fitted mouthguard helps protect teeth from impact and can prevent injuries that may otherwise require treatments such as bonding or crowns.

How does orthodontics fit into my overall dental care?

Orthodontic treatment works best when it is part of a broader dental care plan. Healthy gums, well-maintained teeth, and routine visits help support the process. Many patients combine orthodontic treatment with other dental services over time. For example, some choose to refine their smile later with cosmetic options such as composite bonding or veneers, once alignment has improved.

Is it time to explore orthodontic treatment?

If you have been thinking about straightening your teeth or improving your bite, an orthodontic consultation can help you understand the available options.
