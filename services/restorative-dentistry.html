Restorative Dentistry in Muizenberg & Strandfontein

A chipped tooth, a cavity that has started to cause discomfort, or a gap where a tooth used to be are more common than many people realise. They can also affect how you eat, speak, and feel about your smile. Restorative dentistry offered by our dentist in Muizenberg and Strandfontein focuses on repairing teeth that have been damaged or replacing teeth that are missing.

What is restorative dentistry?

Restorative dentistry includes treatments that rebuild or replace teeth that have been affected by decay, fractures, or wear.

You might need restorative care if you notice:

  • Sensitivity when eating or drinking
  • A tooth that has cracked or chipped
  • A visible cavity
  • A missing tooth that affects chewing

These problems can sometimes begin quietly. A small cavity may not cause pain immediately. That is why routine visits and a scale and polish appointment often play an important role in identifying issues early.

When a concern is found, restorative treatment focuses on repairing the tooth in a way that allows you to continue using it comfortably.

How do fillings repair a tooth?

Fillings are commonly used when a cavity forms in the tooth. The damaged portion of the tooth is removed, and the space is restored with a durable filling material.

A filling helps:

  • Restore the natural shape of the tooth
  • Prevent further decay from spreading
  • Allow you to chew comfortably again

When treated early, a filling can be a simple solution that prevents the need for more complex procedures later. During your appointment, Dr Kamalie may also check the surrounding teeth to ensure that no additional areas require attention.

When is a crown recommended?

Sometimes a tooth becomes too weakened to support a filling alone. This may happen after extensive decay or a fracture that affects a larger portion of the tooth. In these situations, a crown may be recommended.

A crown covers the visible part of the tooth and helps restore its strength and structure. It can allow you to continue using the tooth without discomfort while protecting what remains of the natural tooth underneath.

Crowns are also sometimes placed after certain treatments involving the inner part of the tooth, which may be addressed during root canal therapy.

What if a tooth is missing?

When a tooth is lost, the surrounding teeth may begin to shift over time. This can affect how your bite feels and how evenly pressure is distributed when chewing.

A bridge is one way to replace a missing tooth. It works by anchoring a replacement tooth to the neighbouring teeth, filling the gap and restoring function.

For some patients, restoring a missing tooth also creates a stronger foundation if they later consider treatments that improve the alignment or appearance of their smile, such as clear aligners or composite bonding.

How can restorative dentistry restore my smile?

Restorative dentistry focuses on helping you keep your teeth working as they should. When teeth function comfortably, everyday activities like eating, speaking, and smiling feel easier.

If you have noticed discomfort, damage, or a missing tooth, it may be time to explore restorative treatment with a dentist in Muizenberg and Strandfontein.
